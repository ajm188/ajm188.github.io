In case you weren't aware (I have not done nearly as much shameless
promotion for this as I meant to), [HackCWRU](hack.cwru.edu) **this
weekend**! Our main HQ is likely buzzing with activity at the
moment, but I've been relegated to an outer check-in area, due to some
security issues for non-Case students. It's lonely and cold out here -
every time someone opens the door, a blast of wintry air comes in - so it's
the perfect opportunity to do some reflection, thinking and writing.

## Goals

Being a volunteer, I probably wouldn't have enough time to devote to a
"real" hackathon project. I'm really okay with this, though. I honestly
don't like the "all-in, work non-stop" attitude that these events tend to
celebrate. I'd much rather spend the little free time I have making a
little bit of progress toward some goals.

**Get my homework done**. This one is kind of a
no-brainer. I'll be here for almost thirty-six hours, so there's really no
excuse for not getting it done.

**Get back into meditation**. [Aaron Neyer](http://www.aarongabriel.us/)
is here and had the great idea to get regular meditation sessions going
during the hackathon. It's been way too long since I was meditating
regularly, and I'm really excited to get back into it.

**Contribute to some open source**. I have a couple of things
in mind here. First, I need to get issues counts down on some of my
repositories. Second, I found [this project](https://github.com/zquestz/s)
the other day. It looks awesome! It even has bash completion!
But, I don't use bash. I use fish because I'm weird. So, another thing I
want to work on is adding fish completion support. Third,
[Katherine](http://www.katherines.website/) at one point brought up the idea
of creating a shared "HackCWRU 2016" playlist on Spotify. If that happens,
then I'm going to try to extend our slackbot to be able to add tracks for us.
I'm really hoping I can get to that.

**Food Cart**! Last year, Aaron Neyer and I made a midnight
snack run all over the hackathon. It was awesome. People's faces lit up
when we burst into their room yelling "snacks!" We definitely need to do
that again.
